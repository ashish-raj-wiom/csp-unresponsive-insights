The cross-cutting story

Whether they open the task or not, the mental model is the same: "Is this in my serviceable area / worth my time?" If not, partners silently ignore it — confident it will re-route back to them.

1. Serviceability mismatch

Leads routed to partners with no line, no power or no coverage at that address. The single largest theme across both cohorts.

2. Router / inventory gaps

Enrolled partners who physically can't install right now for lack of a router. Strongest signal in Cohort B.

3. Looping bookings + weak decline/cancel UX

The same booking recirculates; partners can't find cancel, decline options don't fit "wrong address", and cancelled leads reappear. This trains partners to ignore rather than act.

4. Pricing / deposit blindness at the door

Partners don't know the plan price, charges or security fee until they reach the customer — who then refuses. Causes drops even when the partner did try.

What separates the two cohorts

Cohort A — deliberate choice

Mostly a decision made after reviewing the lead: cherry-pick, don't recall, busy, feels no urgency because it loops back. Needs better lead-matching and urgency mechanics.

Cohort B — structural inability

Mostly can't act: no router, no coverage, doesn't understand the app. Needs inventory support, serviceability filtering and onboarding.

Recommended actions

  1. Solve the routing problem. CSPs should only receive serviceable bookings — leads in their actual coverage area. Otherwise they simply don't respond.
  2. Fix the decline/cancel flow. Add a "wrong address / not my area" decline reason and make the cancel button findable; stop cancelled bookings from re-looping unchanged.
  3. Bring a router-ordering flow live on the CSP app. Let partners order/re-stock routers in-app so a missing router no longer blocks installs.
  4. Hand-hold CSPs who don't understand the app. Ops-led support for partners who can't yet navigate the app or tell install vs service tickets apart.
